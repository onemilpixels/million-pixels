# 🕵️ COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE FRAMEWORK

## COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE

There's no direct competitor (Million Pixels is a novel format), but we compete with:
1. **Cheap ad formats** for entrepreneurs/marketers (their budget)
2. **NFT / digital collectibles** (their wallet/mindshare)
3. **Viral marketing courses/communities** (their attention)
4. **Other internet history projects** (legacy nostalgia traffic)

## TIER 1 INDIRECT COMPETITORS (Monitor weekly)

### Reddit Ads
- **Why they matter:** Similar audience, similar price point ($1-5 CPM)
- **Monitor:** Top promoted posts in r/Entrepreneur, r/SideProject
- **Learn:** Title formats, body length, image style

### Indie Hackers / Product Hunt launches
- **Why they matter:** Same audience (early adopters, entrepreneurs)
- **Monitor:** Weekly top launches, their growth tactics
- **Learn:** Hook formulas, social proof patterns

### NFT projects (low-end)
- **Why they matter:** "Own a piece of internet" framing
- **Monitor:** OpenSea trending, NFT Twitter
- **Learn:** Urgency mechanics, community building

### Viral side-hustle creators
- **Why they matter:** Same TikTok / Twitter audience
- **Targets:** @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @arvidkahl, @sergiomattei, etc.
- **Learn:** Storytelling structure, audience engagement patterns

## WHAT TO LOOK FOR

### Creative Patterns That Work
- Opening hook types (question, claim, visual)
- Use of UGC vs polished
- Length (15s, 30s, 60s+)
- CTA placement (front, middle, end)
- Captions/text on screen
- Music choices

### Targeting Hints
- Where competitor ads appear (which subreddits, which TikTok niches)
- Geographic concentration
- Time-of-day patterns

### Pricing/Offer Angles
- How they frame price (per unit, per package, "starting at")
- Urgency mechanics (countdown, scarcity, price increase)
- Trust signals (testimonials, press, guarantees)

## RESPONSE PLAYBOOK

**If a copycat emerges:**
1. Document their launch (screenshots, ad creative, pricing)
2. Assess threat (audience overlap, traffic, virality)
3. Counter-strategy:
   - Lean into "original" / "first-mover" angle
   - Outspend in their key channels if threat is real
   - Public "we're the real one" content (with proof: launch date, customer count)

**If a competitor's ad goes viral:**
1. Reverse-engineer their hook within 24h
2. Create our own variant (different enough to not copy, same psychological lever)
3. Test it in our worst-performing ad set first

**If a competitor gets press:**
1. Pitch the same outlet within 48h with "the bigger story"
2. Comment / engage publicly with the article
3. Reach out to the journalist with exclusive angle

## BRAND DEFENSE (Critical for Phase 2+)

**Always run:**
- Google Search ads on `[million pixels]` brand term (~$10/day)
- TikTok branded hashtag protection (claim official hashtag)
- Twitter brand mention monitoring
- Trademark/domain monitoring (millionpixels.com variants)

**Risk:** Bigger players (Shopify, Squarespace, Wix) could launch a "buy a pixel" feature once we prove the model. Strategy: build community moat fast (Discord, customer loyalty, brand association).
